

Luke Skywalker is a fictional character and the main protagonist of the original Star Wars trilogy. He is portrayed by English actor Mark Hamill in the original trilogy and by Daisy Ridley in the sequel trilogy. Luke is a pivotal figure in the Rebel Alliance's struggle against the Galactic Empire. He trains with his mentor, Obi-Wan Kenobi, and eventually joins the Rebellion, becoming a master Jedi knight and a crucial hero in the Battle of Endor.

Throughout his life, Luke struggles to come to terms with his relationship with his father, the fallen Jedi Anakin Skywalker, a.k.a. Darth Vader. He is also the grand-uncle of Rey, the protagonist of the sequel trilogy. Luke is the son of Padm√© Amidala and Anakin Skywalker, the latter of whom went on to become the Sith Lord Darth Vader.

Luke is introduced in Star Wars Episode IV: A New Hope. After learning of the Death Star's destructive capabilities from Obi-Wan Kenobi, Luke joins forces with Han Solo, Chewbacca, C-3PO and R2-D2 to rescue Princess Leia from the Empire and destroy the Death Star. During the Battle of Yavin, Luke uses the Force to Redirect a proton torpedo into a thermal exhaust port, destroying the battle station. 

After the Battle of Yavin, Luke embarks on a journey to become a Jedi and confront Darth Vader. He is trained by Yoda on Dagobah, where he learns the ways of the Force and defeats Darth Vader in a lightsaber duel. After destroying the Imperial forces in the Battle of Endor, Luke redeems Vader, who dies in his arms. He then celebrates the Alliance's victory with his friends. 

In the years that follow, Luke founds a new Jedi Order and works to restore balance to the Force. He eventually passes on his knowledge to Rey, who completes her training and is eventually declared a Jedi Knight. Luke's legacy lives on in the Force, and he is remembered by the galaxy as a hero and an inspiration.
